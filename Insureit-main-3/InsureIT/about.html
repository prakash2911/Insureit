Features

Many organizations have implemented insurance chatbots, but not all of them are up to standard. They have limited functionality and might be time consuming to use. Customers still need to speak with a real agent in the end. The best virtual assistants give guidance and go above and beyond what a FAQ chatbot can do. To do so, they must understand what customers want, completely comprehend the services provided by the company, and be able to learn from real data in order to communicate with customers and engage in human-like behavior.

Conversations

The most natural mode of human communication is speech. It can be done in a variety of languages, both formally and informally, and these differences must be taken into account when humans and bots communicate. Virtual assistants must be able to understand the context in which they are used. A chatbot should be able to grasp it if an insurance advisor can.

Reliabilty

Insurance firms must be reliable to earn the trust of their customers. This transcends to the technologies they use. Errors in interpretation or transactions are unacceptable and can lead to the loss of a client. Conversational AI InsureIT chatbot is able to prove that it can be trusted.

Multi-Channel

Customers can now use their preferred channel to complete transactions, whether it's a phone call, a mobile app, a smart home device, or a messaging network like WhatsApp or Skype. The finest conversational AI built InsureIT platform helps you to port apps across numerous platforms.

Security

We keep conversational data into a centralized authentication system and is kept private from third parties. Regulations such as GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is followed, allowing dialogues to be consulted, extracted, anonymized, encrypted, or deleted as needed.

Prevent Fraud

Insurance companies must protect the privacy and security of their customers' personal information. InsureIT are effective at monitoring and identifying symptoms of fraud, and they can notify both the insurer and the customer.
